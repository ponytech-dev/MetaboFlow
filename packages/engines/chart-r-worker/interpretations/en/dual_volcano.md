# Dual-Contrast Volcano Comparison

## Description

The dual-contrast volcano comparison displays two independent pairwise contrasts' volcano plots side-by-side (or as a mirror image) to directly compare the overlap and divergence of differential metabolites between the two contrasts. For example, displaying "disease vs. healthy control" alongside "treatment vs. disease" simultaneously can reveal how treatment reverses disease-associated metabolic changes.

## How to Read

- **Side-by-side comparison**: Compare the positions and counts of significant points in both plots; metabolites significant in both plots at the same position are consistently differential across both conditions
- **Mirror mode (back-to-back)**: Commonly used to display the symmetrical relationship between "disease induction" and "treatment reversal" — ideal results show mirror-image symmetry between the two plots
- **Overlapping differential metabolites**: Metabolites significant in both contrasts with consistent direction are the core differential biomarkers
- **Contrast-specific metabolites**: Metabolites significant in only one contrast reflect unique biological responses specific to that condition
- **Color consistency**: Both plots use the same color scheme (red = up-regulated, blue = down-regulated) to facilitate cross-referencing

## Important Notes

- The reference groups for the two contrasts may differ (A vs B and A vs C) — directional interpretation must be clearly labeled
- When both contrasts share the same control group, directional comparison of differential metabolites is more straightforward
- Side-by-side layout is suitable when many differential metabolites are present; mirror layout is better for highlighting symmetric reversal effects
- A Venn diagram between the two plots can be added to quantify overlapping differential metabolites
